This is a small, slow blog about software craft.

I am Marko — a developer based somewhere with poor coffee and good light. Most of my work is web tooling: small CMS engines, plugin systems, and the kind of unglamorous infrastructure that is invisible when it is doing its job.

I started writing here because I noticed the same arguments coming up in code reviews, design meetings, and architecture discussions, and I wanted to put them somewhere I could link to instead of repeating myself. Some of the posts are persuasive; most are descriptive. A few are confessional.

There are no comments, no analytics, and no email capture. If something here is wrong or you want to argue about it, write me a letter at [hello@example.com](mailto:hello@example.com). I read everything and reply when I can.

## Colophon

This site is built on a small flat-file CMS I write in my spare time. There is no database, no JavaScript framework on the public side, and no third-party tracking. Pages are Markdown files on disk. The theme is called *Clean* and it is roughly four hundred lines of CSS.

If you are curious how it works, the [source is on GitHub](https://github.com/krstivoja/mdframework).
